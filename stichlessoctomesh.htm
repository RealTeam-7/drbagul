Stitchless Octomesh Surgery

What is a ventral hernia?

Ventral Hernia or “Abdominal Hernia” occurs when the intestine bulges out due to a weakness of the connective tissue of the abdominal wall. A visit to our Speciality Hernia Care Clinic or to your physician is essential to diagnose this hernia. It affects both men and women.

What are the different types of Ventral hernia?

Ventral hernias have different names - Umbilical hernias (around the belly button), Epigastric hernias (above the stomach) and incisional hernia (around a previous surgery scar/ incisions)

How does ventral hernia feel like?

Hernia can be a small lump without pain or large (as with most hernias); andpainful with severe discomfort, especially during physical activity.

Why does ventral hernia happen?

Incisional hernias following surgery are caused due to healing disorders (collagen weakness or diabetes). Epigastric and umbilical hernias are caused due to stress leading to small rupture of the abdominal muscles or due to abdominal wall weakness since birth.

What is the treatment for ventral hernia?

Wait and watch" approach only increases the complications of hernia. Surgery is the only option to treat abdominal hernias.

What are the options for ventral hernia surgery?

Keyhole or Laparoscopic Surgery

Laparoscopically trained Surgeonmakes3-4 keyholes to place instruments (trocars) into the abdomen. With a video camera and special laparoscopic instruments they repair hernia with mesh and staples (tacks).

Open Surgery

This is the most common method. Surgeon makes an incision (cut) above the hernia in the abdomen. They repair the hernia with mesh and sutures (stitches).

What is the difference between open and laparoscopic surgery?

Keyhole surgery:


Risks

Technically challenging
Carried out under general anaesthesia at a specialist laparoscopic centre with sophisticated laparoscopic instruments
Internal organ damage

Benefits

Faster return to work / normal activity after the surgery Suitable for patients with smaller, less complex ventral hernias or recurrent hernias

Open surgery :


Benefits

More frequently carried out as ventral hernias are larger and complex Cost-effective (cost of laparoscopic is 3X that of open surgery)

Risk

Associated with major abdominal incisions More painful, but modern pain management techniques can help greatly

What is “No Stitch and No Tack” Hernia treatment?

Hernia Mesh or Hernia Implant which is used to treat the hernia hole has to be stitched around the site of hernia. The use of mesh has reduced the incidence of recurrence but stitching it to tissues/ muscles/ nerves increases in the incidence of chronic pain.
Stitchless Tension-free Hernia Surgery is a Modern surgery which involves repair through selection of specially designed Octomesh Implant. Shaped like Octopus – it has body with eight arms. The arms are tunnelled into the muscles using a special instrument. This helps to hold the implant in place without the need of any sutures/ stitches /staples/ tacks/ glue hence considered TRULY TENSION-FREE.
