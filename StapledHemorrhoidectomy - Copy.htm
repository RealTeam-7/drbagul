Stapled Hemorrhoidectomy

What is Hemorrhoidectomy?

Hemorrhoidectomy is surgery to remove hemorrhoids done under anesthesia (general or spinal) so that patient will not feel pain. Surgery is done in DayCare where patient gets discharged the same day.


How is Hemorrhoidectomy done?

Surgery is recommended for large internal hemorrhoids and external hemorrhoids. Small internal hemorrhoids improve with medications.
In traditional surgery, surgeon makes incisions around the hemorrhoid. The swollen vein inside the hemorrhoid is tied off or cut to prevent bleeding and the hemorrhoid is removed. The surgical area may be stitched or left open and wound is covered.
Lasers are often advertised as a painless, faster-healing method of treating hemorrhoids. But none of these claims have been proved. Lasers are expensive than traditional techniques, procedure takes longer, and it may cause deep tissue injury.

What is Stapled Hemorrhoidectomy?

This is a new surgical technique to return the hemorrhoids to a normal position inside the rectum.
Surgeon uses a circular stapling device to lift the hemorrhoid, “staple" it back into place in the anal canal and close the wound. This is a minimal invasive surgery as there is no need of taking incisions.Patients have less pain after surgery than the traditional hemorrhoid surgery. Complete recovery takes about 2 to 3 weeks. It is a method for choice for grade 3 hemorrhoids or higher.

What to expect after Stapled Hemorrhoidectomy?

Anesthesia given during surgery will provide pain relief for 6 to 12 hours after surgery. Moderate pain after-surgery is a common event. Antibiotics, stool-softeners/laxatives and pain-killer medications will be prescribed for a week to help combat any infection, relieve pain after bowel movement, prevent constipation and any straining at bowel evacuation to avoid hemorrhoids recurring. Some bleeding is normal, especially with the first bowel movement after surgery. You will be advised bland diet and plenty of fluids for few weeks gradually transiting to regular foods rich in fiber content. Follow-up visit will be scheduled a month after surgery.

What are the side-effects of Stapled Hemorrhoidectomy?

Stapled Hemorrhoidectomy is considered as a safe procedure. Relatively minor side-effects such as moderate levels of pain, bleeding and urinary retention are the most common side-effects which resolve without intervention. Rare but more serious side-effects include Hemorrhoid recurrence or Fistula or Prolapse (part of rectum protrudes out of the anus).


What is your role in the success of Stapled Hemorrhoidectomy?

Successful hemorrhoidectomy surgery depends a lot on your attention to make changes in lifestyle, dietary and bowel habits.
